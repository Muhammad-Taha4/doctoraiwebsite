7 Billing Mistakes That Are Silently Costing Your Practice Thousands

By Cure Claim Solutions Team January 15, 2024 8 min read

Medical practices lose an estimated $125 billion annually to billing errors, denials, and missed charges — and most practices don't even know it's happening. The billing process is complex, and small mistakes compound over time into significant revenue loss.

We've audited hundreds of medical practices, and we see the same errors over and over again. Here are the seven most costly billing mistakes — and exactly how to fix them.

Mistake #1

Not Verifying Patient Eligibility Before the Visit

The most preventable source of claim denials. When providers don't verify coverage upfront, they risk treating patients with lapsed policies, wrong plans, or no coverage at all — leading to a denied claim they must write off or chase from the patient.

The Fix: Verify eligibility 24–48 hours before every single appointment. Confirm active coverage, deductible status, co-pay amounts, and authorization requirements.
Mistake #2

Incorrect or Incomplete ICD-10 Coding

Using outdated codes, unspecified codes when specific ones exist, or linking codes incorrectly to procedures is one of the leading causes of denials. ICD-10 has over 70,000 codes and payers require precise, current coding with full specificity.

The Fix: Use CPC-certified coders who specialize in your specialty and complete annual code update training. Code audits quarterly to catch patterns before payers do.
Mistake #3

Not Appealing Denied Claims

Studies show that up to 65% of denied claims are never appealed — and of those that are appealed, about 50–70% are overturned in the provider's favor. Practices that don't appeal are leaving massive amounts of owed money behind.

The Fix: Implement a systematic denial management process. Every denial should be reviewed within 24 hours of receipt and assigned for appeal before payer deadlines.

Mistake #4

Missing Modifiers or Using the Wrong Ones

CPT modifiers provide critical context about a service — whether it's bilateral, performed by a different provider, or part of a global period. Missing or incorrect modifiers result in underpayments, denials, and NCCI edit failures.

The Fix: Maintain a modifier guide specific to your specialty and payer mix. Review all modifier edits annually as payer policies change.
Mistake #5

Failing to Submit Claims on Time

Every payer has a timely filing deadline — often 90–365 days from the date of service. Once that window closes, the claim is denied with no ability to appeal, and the revenue is permanently lost.

The Fix: Aim to submit all claims within 5–7 business days of service. Build a charge lag report into your weekly monitoring to catch any delays immediately.
Mistake #6

Accepting Underpayments Without Questioning Them

Payers make payment mistakes too. Studies show that 7–10% of claims are paid below contracted rates. Most practices never check, and these underpayments become permanent write-offs when not pursued within the reconsideration window.

The Fix: Implement a payment variance report that flags any EOB payment below contracted rates. Dispute all underpayments within payer-specified timeframes.
Mistake #7

Maintaining Poor Patient Payment Processes

Even after a claim is paid correctly by insurance, many practices struggle to collect the patient's portion. Unclear billing statements, no payment plan options, and delayed statements lead to uncollected balances that age into bad debt.

The Fix: Collect co-pays and known balances at time of service. Send patient statements within 14 days of payment posting. Offer online payment and payment plans.
