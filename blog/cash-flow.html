How to Improve Your Practice Cash Flow With Better Medical Billing

By Cure Claim Solutions Team January 22, 2024 6 min read

Cash flow is the lifeblood of any medical practice. Yet for many providers, the gap between services rendered and payment received is frustratingly wide — sometimes stretching to 45, 60, or even 90+ days. The good news: most cash flow problems in medical practices are billing problems in disguise, and they're fixable.

45–60 daysAvg. payment cycle with poor billing
14–21 daysPayment cycle with optimized billing
30%+Revenue increase possible with RCM improvements

1. Reduce Your Days in Accounts Receivable (AR)

The single biggest driver of cash flow problems is a bloated AR. Industry benchmarks suggest your AR days should stay under 35 days. If you're routinely above 45, there are billing process failures driving that number up.

How to Fix It: Submit Claims Faster

Implement same-day or next-day charge entry for all encounters. Every day a claim sits unsubmitted is a day you're not getting paid. Target a charge lag of less than 48 hours.

2. Maximize Your First-Pass Claim Acceptance Rate

Every claim that gets denied delays your cash flow by weeks or months while you appeal. Each re-work cycle adds administrative cost on top of the payment delay. The goal is to get it right the first time.

Target: 95%+ First-Pass Rate

This requires complete eligibility verification before every visit, accurate coding with all required modifiers, correct patient and payer information, and clean claim scrubbing before submission. Use pre-submission claim edits to catch issues before they cause denials.

Industry BenchmarkHigh-performing practices achieve 95–98% first-pass acceptance. The average practice is around 85–88%. Moving from 85% to 95% can add $20,000–$50,000 to annual revenue in a mid-size practice.

3. Establish a Systematic Denial Management Process

Denials are inevitable — it's how quickly and effectively you handle them that determines your cash flow impact. Every denial should be triaged within 24 hours, and high-value denials should be appealed as a priority within the first week. Set a 30-day target for all denial resolutions.

Track Denial Reasons by Category

Build a denial tracking report that categorizes denials by reason code, payer, and dollar value. This reveals patterns that indicate systemic billing errors you can fix at the root — not just claim-by-claim.

4. Improve Patient Collections at the Point of Service

As patient cost-sharing increases, patient collections have become a major revenue cycle challenge. High-deductible plans mean patients owe a larger share of every visit — and collecting that money becomes increasingly difficult after they leave your office.

Collect Co-pays and Known Balances Before the Visit Ends

With verified eligibility data in hand before each appointment, your front desk knows exactly what the patient owes. A payment policy that collects at time of service — combined with multiple payment method options — dramatically improves patient collection rates.

5. Pursue Aged Receivables Aggressively

Claims sitting in the 90–120+ day AR bucket are at serious risk of becoming uncollectable. Payer timely reconsideration limits, timely appeal deadlines, and patient collection difficulty all increase with age. Don't let old AR sit — work it before it expires.

Run a monthly report of all AR over 60 days and prioritize it for immediate action. Assign dedicated staff or engage a billing company with proven expertise in AR recovery. The recovery rates on well-worked aged AR can be surprisingly high — and the revenue is already earned.

The Bottom Line

Better billing is the fastest path to better cash flow — no new patients, no new services, no new equipment required. The opportunity is hiding in your current claims data, AR report, and denial logs. All it takes is the expertise and processes to capture it.
